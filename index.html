Sarah & Gabe are Getting Married

October 4th, 2014

The day I proposed was a hot, muggy and overcast New England day. It was a normal day that was made unforgettable with the help of our little puppy, Sweet Pea and an Opal necklace.

After I got home from work, I kissed and hugged Sarah as I always do, then I picked up Sweet Pea who was jumping up and down at my legs by this point. The routine is get home, kiss Sarah, then take Sweets out to do her business.

I grabbed her leash and headed outside. We did the regular loop around the block, accept this time I stopped back at my car before we went back in. I grabbed the necklace from the glove compartment.

At this point I have to say, I had no idea what I was doing. For the month or so prior to this, I was thinking of cool ways to propose to Sarah. "Red-Sox game." was one thought... Do I really like the Red Sox that much? No. not really. I can only name one player. Top of a mountain. Okay. Well, Actually, can a British-Columbian really propose on top of a mountain in Massachusetts? No. it doesn't feel right. What about some sort of trip somewhere? But where would be worthy of such an event?

Before I knew it, I had spent a month thinking about "Cool" ways to propose. At that point I decided that this wasn't about the act of proposing, proposing is just the begining. We'll climb plenty of mountains (real ones), go to plenty of Sox games, travel, and go on plenty of adventures together. the act of asking her to spend the rest of her life with me is beautiful in itself. I'm not waiting any longer. So I said, "Im doing it today! It'll be magical!"

So I grabbed Sweet Pea and loosely wrapped the necklace around her neck. "Sarah! Sweet Pea got into some trash! there's something around her neck!" I yelled to Sarah from the porch. I should also mention that Sarah is absolutely crazy about her puppy.

"Is she alright?", She said as she crashed through the screen door.

I watched Sarah closely as she grabbed the dog from me. "What the? What is this?" she said as she unraveled the Necklace from the puppies neck. She looked at it and I waited. She had told me that she wanted an Opal necklace (her birthstone) as engagement jewelry. I waited for her to get it.

"Lindsay! I think the dog got into your room!". Sarah thought that the necklace belonged to our roommate, Lindsay. So my on-the-spot plan backfired a little bit.

I touched Sarah's arm, "No. It's yours. I got it for you."

She looked at me, puzzled by how serious I had become (something I rarely do), and said "I don't have one like this..." then she got it. I'll never forget the look on her face. She put her hands over her mouth as I got down on one knee and asked.

"Will you marry me?"

She said yes. Then we went out for some italian and ice cream. An ordinary day I'll never forget.

The End

...or is it

...no. it's just the begining like I said earlier. See you in October.
